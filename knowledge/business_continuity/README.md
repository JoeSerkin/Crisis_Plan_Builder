# Business Continuity Knowledge

Reference materials for aligning crisis management with business continuity planning (BCP).

## Core concepts

- **Business Impact Analysis (BIA)** — Identifies critical activities, dependencies, and maximum tolerable downtime.
- **Recovery objectives** — RTO (recovery time) and RPO (recovery point) set expectations for restart and data loss.
- **Minimum operating requirements** — Staff, systems, and third parties needed to sustain priority activities during disruption.

## Crisis–BCP integration

| Phase | BCP role |
|-------|----------|
| Activation | Crisis team confirms which BCP plans apply and assigns recovery leads |
| Stabilization | Alternate sites, manual workarounds, and supplier contingencies execute |
| Recovery | Sequenced restart against pre-defined priorities and customer commitments |
| Stand-down | Transition from crisis command to sustained operations and plan updates |

## Consultant prompts

- Which activities are truly critical vs. deferrable during a multi-day outage?
- Are BCP plans tested at the same cadence as crisis exercises?
- Do vendor BCP assessments cover tier-1 suppliers with contractual notification SLAs?
