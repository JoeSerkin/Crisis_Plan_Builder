# Executive Decision-Making Knowledge

Reference materials for crisis governance, authority, and board engagement.

## Decision principles during crisis

- **Speed with accountability** — Pre-delegated authorities prevent delays when executives are unavailable.
- **Single crisis director** — One accountable leader sets objectives; others advise and execute.
- **Documented rationale** — Decision logs support legal privilege, insurance, and post-incident review.

## Board and executive engagement

| Crisis level | Typical board role |
|--------------|-------------------|
| Level 1–2 | Informed; no standing meeting required |
| Level 3 | Executive committee briefed; board chair notified |
| Level 4 | Full board protocol; material disclosure assessment |

## Consultant prompts

- What financial and operational authorities are pre-delegated before activation?
- When does the board chair join the crisis call vs. receive written briefings?
- Are external advisors (legal, PR, security) on retainer with defined activation triggers?
